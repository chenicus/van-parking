Privacy

Park Daddy has no accounts and no logins. I don't want your personal information and I don't collect it.

What stays on your device

Your location and your recent searches never leave your browser. Location is used to draw the map and work out drive times, then it's gone — it isn't sent anywhere or stored.

What I do collect

  • Anonymous usage stats: pages viewed, which buttons get pressed, which city you're browsing, and the approximate city you're visiting from (worked out from your IP address, which is discarded and never stored).
  • If you search for somewhere Park Daddy doesn't cover, the town, state or province, and country (for example "Portland, Oregon") so I know where to expand next. Never the address you typed.
  • Anything you type into a report or the feedback form, plus any photo you attach. That's only what you choose to send.

There are no ads here, and I don't sell or share any of this with anyone.

Other services this app talks to

Loading a map and searching means your browser fetches things from other companies, who will see your IP address as a result:

  • CARTO and MapLibre: map tiles and styling
  • OpenStreetMap (Nominatim, Valhalla) and Komoot (Photon): search, autocomplete and directions. Places you search for are sent to these to look up.
  • Cloudflare and PostHog: the anonymous usage stats above
  • Supabase: stores reports and feedback
  • ArcGIS: live parking availability in Kirkland

Your choices

Turning on "Do Not Track" or Global Privacy Control in your browser switches the usage stats off. Clearing your browser data wipes your saved searches. Want something you sent deleted? Just ask.

Kids

This isn't aimed at children under 13, and I don't knowingly collect anything from them.
